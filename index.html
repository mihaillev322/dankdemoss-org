Key terms defined

Doctrine
A set of beliefs or principles held and taught by a religious organization or theological teacher. In Christian context, doctrine refers to systematic statements about God, scripture, salvation, and church practice derived from biblical interpretation.
Theology
The systematic study of the nature of God, religious belief, and the practice of faith. Christian theology examines scripture, tradition, reason, and experience to articulate coherent understanding of divine truth and its application to human life.
Sermons
Formal religious discourses delivered by clergy or preachers, typically based on biblical texts and intended to instruct, inspire, or challenge congregations in their faith practice and spiritual understanding.
